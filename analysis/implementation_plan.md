## Maintaining Ambiguity (How to Never Confirm)

### Techniques to Make Readers *Sense* Nagare Knows:

**1. Nagare's Too-Perfect Awareness**
- He knows things he shouldn't (Iwafune's specific guilt patterns)
- His manipulation targets Iwafune's exact weak spots
- He's too confident Iwafune won't leave

**2. Moments of Almost-Revealing**
- Nagare starts to say something, then stops
- Looks at Iwafune with "knowing" expression
- References "shared past" vaguely

**3. Iwafune's Paranoia**
- Wonders "does he know?"
- Catches Nagare looking at him strangely
- Never asks directly (too afraid of answer)

**4. Dialogue That Works Both Ways**
- Lines that mean one thing if Nagare doesn't know
- Mean something MUCH deeper if he does
- Reader can interpret either way
