## Story Overview

**Genre**: Psychological thriller with horror elements
**POV**: Third person limited (Iwafune)
**Tone**: More external/action-oriented than main story, but still intimate
**Length**: ~8,000-10,000 words
**Timeline**: Spans ~6-8 years (catastrophe ‚Üí pre-Prologue)

**Purpose**:

- Explain parasite acquisition (infiltration)
- Maintain Nagare's ambiguity (never his POV)
- Add action/thriller elements (catastrophe, infiltration)

## ACT 1: THE FACILITY (~1,200 words)

### Purpose
- Establish Iwafune's pre-catastrophe character
- Show his relationship with the facility
- Introduce the outbreak threat
- Set up his moral framework (follows orders, trusts authority)

### Setting
- Research facility (underground? remote?)
- Clinical, sterile, institutional
- Iwafune in field operative role

### Scenes

#### Scene 1.1: Morning Briefing (300 words)
**Location**: Facility briefing room
**Characters**: Iwafune, Commander/Superior, other operatives

**Key moments**:
- Iwafune receives assignment: outbreak in [City Name]
- Details: Parasite infection, spreading rapidly
- Orders: Contain, quarantine, assess survivors
- Iwafune's mindset: This is what I signed up for

**Dialogue**:
> "Containment protocol is absolute. No unauthorized exits from the quarantine zone."
>
> Iwafune nodded. He'd followed orders for six years. This would be no different.

**Establish**:
- Iwafune as "good soldier"
- Facility as morally grey (clinical about human cost)
- His belief in the mission (naive idealism)

---

#### Scene 1.2: Preparation (200 words)
**Location**: Iwafune's quarters / Equipment room

**Key moments**:
- Packing gear (hazmat, weapons, samples kits)
- Brief character reflection
- Last normal moment before everything changes

**Internal thought**:
> Six years he'd worked for them. Six years of believing they were protecting humanity from the parasites. He'd seen the infected‚Äîthe ones who'd lost themselves to the organisms. He'd never questioned the necessity of containment.
>
> Not yet.

**Foreshadowing**: "Not yet"

---

#### Scene 1.3: The Mission Begins (700 words)
**Location**: En route to city / Arrival at perimeter

**Key moments**:
- Travel to outbreak site
- First signs of catastrophe (smoke on horizon)
- Arrival at quarantine perimeter
- Reality worse than briefing suggested

**Sensory details**:
- Smoke visible miles away
- Radio chatter (panicked, fragmented)
- Checkpoint chaos (soldiers, refugees trying to escape)

>
> This was annihilation.

**Tension building**:
- City already lost
- Infection spread faster than anticipated
- Survivors trapped inside
- Orders unchanged: Contain. Document. No rescues.

**End of Act 1**: Iwafune enters the dead city.

- Show the horror of the outbreak (visceral, intense)

### Scenes

**Key moments**:

>

#### Scene 2.2: The Survivor Pocket (600 words)
**Location**: Makeshift shelter (school? community center?)

**Key moments**:
- Iwafune finds group of survivors (20-30 people)

**Characters**:

#### Scene 2.3: The Boy in the Wreckage (900 words)
**Location**: Ruined residential area

**Key moments**:
- Iwafune extracting from city
- Finds child (Nagare, age 6-8) in wreckage
- Boy is alive, alone, silent
- The moment that will haunt him

**Nagare's appearance**:
- Small, frail, covered in ash
- Blue eyes watching (unnervingly calm)
- Not crying, not screaming
- Just... observing

**The encounter**:
- Iwafune approaches
- Checks boy for infection (negative)
- Boy doesn't speak
- Watches Iwafune with those eyes

**Critical moment**:
> The boy tilted his head, studying Iwafune's face through the gas mask's visor. Brown eyes met blue.
>
> Iwafune crouched beside him. "What's your name?"
>
> No answer. Just that steady, unsettling gaze.
>
> "I‚Äî" Iwafune's radio crackled. "Operative, report status. Withdraw immediately. Quarantine perimeter closing in ten minutes."
>
> Ten minutes. He could carry the boy out. Could save one life from this hell.
>
> "Operative, confirm."
>
> Iwafune's hand hovered over the boy's shoulder.

**The choice** (parallel to survivor pocket):
- Save the boy = disobey, risk infection spread, become fugitive
- Leave the boy = follow orders, survive, go home
- He's already left the others
- What's one more?

**The moment**:
> "I'm sorry," Iwafune whispered.
>
> The boy didn't react. Didn't beg. Didn't cry.
>
> Just watched as Iwafune stood. Turned. Walked away.
>
> Behind him, a small voice, barely audible: "Okay."
>
> Iwafune didn't look back.

**Iwafune's rationalization**:
- Boy might be infected (incubating)
- Orders are orders
- He's not a monster (is he?)
- Someone else will help
- It's not his responsibility

**Truth**: He's afraid. Afraid of consequences. Afraid of choosing.

**Last image**:
> Iwafune reached the perimeter as the explosions began. Containment protocol. They were burning the city. Burning the infected. Burning the survivors.
>
> Burning a small boy with blue eyes who'd said "Okay" when abandoned.
>
> Iwafune vomited inside his mask.

**End of Act 2**: Iwafune escapes. The city burns. Nagare presumably dies.

## Writing Guidelines

### Tone Differences from Main Story:

**Main Story**:
- Intimate, contained
- Psychological horror
- Internal focus
- Slow, oppressive
- Two people, one room

**Companion Piece**:
- External events matter
- Action/thriller elements
- World-building allowed
- Faster pace (covers years)
- Multiple locations

**BUT maintain**:
- Iwafune's internal guilt
- Intimate emotional moments
- Nagare's mystery
- Psychological depth
- Same prose style

### Prose Style:
- Match main story's voice
- Shorter paragraphs for action
- Longer paragraphs for guilt/reflection
- Sensory details (especially catastrophe)
- Iwafune's internal narration

### What to SHOW:
- The catastrophe (visceral, horrifying)
- Iwafune's choice to obey
- Years of guilt
- Orphanage dynamic
- Infiltration (tense, exciting)

### What to HINT:
- Nagare's awareness
- Facility's true nature
- Why outbreak happened
- Iwafune's exact role

### What to NEVER REVEAL:
- Nagare's thoughts
- Whether he remembers
- His manipulation strategy (if any)
- Full facility conspiracy
